## Self-Destruction as Character Study

*Raging Bull* is one of Scorsese's most severe films because it strips away the romance of sporting success and focuses instead on jealousy, shame, ego, and self-punishment. Jake LaMotta is not framed as an inspiring fighter undone by circumstances. He is shown as a man who repeatedly helps engineer his own isolation.

The boxing scenes are extraordinary, but they do not glorify competition. They externalize inner violence. The ring becomes a place where fury, humiliation, and self-loathing are stylized into impact.

## Why the Film Feels So Unforgiving

Robert De Niro's performance gives the film its terrifying center. Physical transformation matters, but the deeper achievement is emotional density: LaMotta's suspicion, need, cruelty, and woundedness remain painfully visible.

*Raging Bull* endures because it understands masculinity as something damaged, performative, and often destructive to the self that inhabits it.
