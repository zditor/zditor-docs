## Romantic Comedy Rewritten as Memory

*Annie Hall* changed the romantic comedy by shifting the focus from whether a couple ends up together to how a relationship is remembered, interpreted, and broken apart. The film uses digression, self-conscious narration, and formal play not as decoration, but as ways of thinking through intimacy.

Diane Keaton's presence is essential to the film's balance. Without her openness, unpredictability, and charm, the movie's self-analysis would risk becoming too closed in on itself.

## Why the Film Feels Modern

Its structure acknowledges that love stories are shaped by reflection after the fact. Humor here is tied to insecurity, projection, and the inability to fully understand another person even while loving them.

*Annie Hall* endures because it treats romance as something shaped by wit, longing, and loss in equal measure.
