## Journalism as Patient Democratic Labor

*All the President's Men* remains one of the strongest films about journalism because it presents reporting not as glamorous revelation, but as repetitive, uncertain, and painstaking work. Calls are made, names are checked, doors are knocked on, and patterns emerge only through persistence.

That procedural discipline gives the film its tension. Robert Redford and Dustin Hoffman are compelling, but the movie's real subject is the process by which democratic institutions try to hold power accountable.

## Why the Film Still Feels Necessary

Its atmosphere of secrecy, caution, and institutional resistance has not faded. The film understands that truth in public life often appears first as fragments that powerful systems work hard to keep disconnected.

*All the President's Men* endures because it treats investigation as civic responsibility rather than heroic spectacle.
