## Precision Comedy on a Monumental Scale

*The General* remains one of the greatest silent comedies because Buster Keaton combines deadpan performance, mechanical elegance, and astonishing physical scale into a film that feels both playful and exact. The trains, tracks, chases, and stunts are not decorative spectacle. They are the grammar of the comedy itself.

Keaton's brilliance lies in the way he treats enormous danger with calm persistence. The humor comes not from panic, but from problem-solving under absurdly escalating conditions.

## Why the Film Still Feels Astonishing

Its action remains vivid because it is grounded in real motion, real space, and rigorous timing. The film's large-scale set pieces never lose clarity, which makes them exciting and funny at once.

*The General* endures because it turns engineering, movement, and cinematic precision into pure entertainment.
