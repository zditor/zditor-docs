## Ambition, Performance, and Female Stardom

*All About Eve* remains one of the sharpest backstage dramas because it understands the theater world as a place where admiration, insecurity, ambition, and cruelty are constantly entangled. The story is not simply about rivalry. It is about performance extending beyond the stage into every social exchange.

Bette Davis gives Margo Channing extraordinary wit and force, which is why the film can explore vanity without flattening her into caricature. Eve's ascent only becomes compelling because Margo is so vivid.

## Why the Film Still Feels So Precise

Its dialogue remains one of its great strengths, but the film's durability comes from its understanding of status anxiety and public image. Recognition is shown as unstable, addictive, and easily manipulated.

*All About Eve* endures because it sees success as theatrical in the deepest sense: something staged, watched, desired, and always at risk.
