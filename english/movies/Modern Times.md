## Comedy Against the Machinery of Modern Life

*Modern Times* remains one of Chaplin's defining works because it transforms industrial modernity into both comic material and social critique. Factories, assembly lines, automation, and surveillance are not treated as abstract issues. They are experienced through the body, which is exactly where Chaplin's genius resides.

The Tramp is again central, but here his vulnerability is sharpened by the pressures of mechanized labor and economic instability. Comedy becomes a way of exposing how efficiency can deform human rhythm and dignity.

## Why the Film Still Feels Fresh

Its satire endures because the anxieties it stages have not disappeared. The fear that people might be reduced to functions, processed by systems, or displaced by speed still feels immediate.

*Modern Times* endures because it proves comedy can be both light on its feet and exact in its political intuition.
