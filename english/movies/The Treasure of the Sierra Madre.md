## Adventure Corroded by Greed

*The Treasure of the Sierra Madre* remains one of the great adventure films because it turns the search for wealth into a study of paranoia, distrust, and self-destruction. The treasure is never just reward. It is an instrument that reveals what fear and greed can do to judgment.

Humphrey Bogart's performance is central to the film's power. His character's deterioration is gradual enough to feel believable and severe enough to make the film darkly unforgettable.

## Why the Film Still Feels Sharp

Its adventure structure gives the story momentum, but the real interest lies in moral erosion. John Huston understands how quickly partnership can decay when scarcity and suspicion begin to govern perception.

*The Treasure of the Sierra Madre* endures because it treats greed not as vice in the abstract, but as a force that reorganizes reality.
