## Hollywood Dream Factory as Gothic Ruin

*Sunset Blvd.* remains one of the sharpest films ever made about Hollywood because Billy Wilder sees the industry not simply as glamorous illusion, but as a machine that produces obsolescence, fantasy, and self-deception. The mansion at the center of the story is both a shrine to past fame and a tomb.

Gloria Swanson's Norma Desmond is unforgettable because the film gives her delusion both grandeur and sadness. She is monstrous in her demands, yet the movie never stops showing how the culture around her helped create the conditions for that madness.

## Why the Film Still Bites

Its cynicism is exact rather than broad. Wilder understands the transactions between youth and age, desperation and vanity, ambition and compromise. William Holden's Joe Gillis is not an innocent observer, which keeps the film morally alive.

*Sunset Blvd.* endures because it treats celebrity as a form of hauntedness. It is a satire, a tragedy, and a ghost story about image all at once.
