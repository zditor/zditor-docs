## Performance Under the Shadow of Fascism

*Cabaret* remains one of the most sophisticated movie musicals because it refuses to let performance float free of politics. Bob Fosse uses the club stage not as escapist refuge, but as a distorting mirror for the anxieties, appetites, and moral evasions of a society moving toward fascism.

Liza Minnelli gives the film its dazzling surface, but the movie's greatness lies in the tension between surface and collapse. Pleasure, decadence, and self-display become increasingly haunted by the world outside the cabaret.

## Why the Film Still Feels Sharp

Its musical numbers do not interrupt the story; they interpret it. That structural intelligence allows the film to connect spectacle with commentary at nearly every turn.

*Cabaret* endures because it understands entertainment as both seduction and warning.
