## Urban Loneliness and Fragile Friendship

*Midnight Cowboy* remains distinctive because it strips away glamour from fantasies of success and reinvention in New York. The city is harsh, transactional, and emotionally cold, and the film lets that environment wear down both illusion and body.

What gives the movie its lasting force is the bond between Joe Buck and Ratso Rizzo. Their relationship is awkward, tender, exploitative, and deeply human, which prevents the film from becoming simply a portrait of social decay.

## Why the Film Still Feels Exposed

Its emotional honesty comes from refusing to romanticize marginality. The characters are lonely, damaged, and often deluded, yet the movie grants them dignity through closeness rather than sentimentality.

*Midnight Cowboy* endures because it sees companionship as precious precisely in a world that makes people disposable.
