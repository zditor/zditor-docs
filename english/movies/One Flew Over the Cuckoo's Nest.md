## Rebellion Against Institutional Power

*One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest* remains gripping because it turns a psychiatric ward into a concentrated model of social control. The conflict between McMurphy and Nurse Ratched is not simply personal. It stages a broader struggle between unruly vitality and systems that define order through humiliation, surveillance, and obedience.

Jack Nicholson gives McMurphy charisma without sanctifying him. That matters because the film is not interested in easy hero worship. His energy is liberating, but it is also reckless, and the ward's fragile community is transformed through both inspiration and danger.

## Why the Tragedy Hits So Hard

The film's emotional force comes from its refusal to offer painless victory. It understands that institutions do not collapse just because someone exposes their cruelty. Resistance carries consequence, and that consequence gives the final movement its severity.

What survives, finally, is not triumph but the possibility of freedom wrested from sacrifice. *One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest* endures because it knows liberation can be real even when it is purchased at unbearable cost.
