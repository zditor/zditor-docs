## Comedy Through Performed Identity

*Tootsie* remains one of the smartest mainstream comedies of its era because it uses disguise not only for farce, but for social observation. The film links performance, employment, ego, and gender expectations in a way that stays funny while still carrying genuine critical weight.

Dustin Hoffman's central performance works because the film never lets Michael's transformation become merely a trick. The experience forces him to confront ways of seeing and behaving that he had previously ignored or excused.

## Why the Film Still Holds Up

Its comedy depends on timing, but its durability comes from insight. The film understands that changing roles can expose structures of everyday condescension and entitlement more effectively than a speech ever could.

*Tootsie* endures because it turns comic impersonation into an argument about attention and respect.
