## Nuclear Anxiety Turned Into Farce

*Dr. Strangelove* remains devastating because Stanley Kubrick understood that the logic of nuclear annihilation had become too irrational to portray straight. Comedy does not soften the horror here; it exposes how bureaucratic language, masculine paranoia, and military procedure can carry civilization toward catastrophe.

Peter Sellers's multiple performances intensify that absurdity by turning authority into instability. Power in the film is never reassuring. It is theatrical, brittle, and fatally detached from consequence.

## Why the Satire Has Not Aged Out

The film sees systems as dangerous not because they malfunction accidentally, but because they can operate exactly as designed and still produce madness. That insight gives the comedy its edge and keeps the movie from feeling merely topical.

*Dr. Strangelove* endures because it converts existential dread into a form of merciless clarity.
