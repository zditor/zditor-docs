## Antiwar Comedy Through Institutional Chaos

*MASH* remains distinctive because it approaches war obliquely, through irreverence, procedural chaos, and gallows humor rather than direct battlefield heroics. Robert Altman uses overlapping action and tonal instability to present military life as absurd, fragmented, and emotionally defensive.

The comedy is frequently abrasive, and that abrasiveness is part of the point. The characters use mockery and disruption as ways of surviving a system whose violence exceeds what ordinary language can manage.

## Why the Film Still Feels Different

Its antiwar force comes from method rather than sermon. The film lets disorder, interruption, and cynicism accumulate until the institution itself begins to look ridiculous and inhuman.

*MASH* endures because it turns tonal messiness into a political style.
