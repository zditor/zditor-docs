## Ambition at the Scale of Early Cinema

*Intolerance* remains historically important because it pushes early cinema toward an enormous narrative architecture. D. W. Griffith intercuts stories from different eras to create a sweeping argument about persecution, fanaticism, and human cruelty across time.

The film's scale is inseparable from its ambition. Sets, crowds, and editing are all mobilized to show cinema striving toward a new kind of epic form. Even when the film feels unwieldy, that restlessness is part of what makes it so revealing.

## Why the Film Still Matters as History

Its significance lies not only in what it achieves, but in what it demonstrates about the medium's early aspirations. It shows film discovering cross-cutting, historical simultaneity, and monumental spectacle as tools for grand moral argument.

*Intolerance* endures because it captures cinema thinking bigger than itself almost from the beginning.
